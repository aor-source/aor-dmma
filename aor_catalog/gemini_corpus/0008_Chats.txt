The Law of Diminishing Returns: Why "More" can be "Worse"

In machine learning, there is a specific threshold where adding more features actually decreases your model's accuracy. This is often called the Hughes Phenomenon.

Data Sparsity: As you add dimensions (features), the volume of your "data space" increases exponentially. Your 300+ features create a massive, mostly empty void where data points become so isolated that the AI can no longer find meaningful relationships between them.

The Overfitting Trap: With 400 features, a model becomes "too powerful" for its own good. It stops learning what a "party anthem" sounds like and starts memorizing the specific background hiss or micro-glitch of a single recording, thinking that's a required feature.

The Loss of Distance: In high-dimensional spaces, traditional ways of measuring "closeness" (like Euclidean distance) lose their meaning. Every song starts looking equally "far" from every other song, making classification nearly impossible.
